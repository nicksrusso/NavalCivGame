# Game

## Goals

This is a strategy game inspired by Sid Meyer's civilization games and the Pacific Theater in WWII. Its a sandbox to implement a Gymnasium/petting zoo compatible environment to test AI agents game playing. 

The game has 2 players and a map consisting of water, land, and ports. A player wins by capturing the enemy home port. Turns are taken sequentially, with each player allowed to choose as many or few moves as desired per turn. 

## Combat 

A port is captured by positioning a LANDING ship on a water tile adjacent to an enemy port and issuing a `CapturePortAction`. Ship vs ship combat is resolved as a binary outcome (ship is alive or dead, no damage model). Each ship type has a cost, strength, attack range, speed, visibility coefficient, and scouting coefficient

A ship can attack any spotted enemy ship within its attack range on a given turn. Ships can move before attacking. When a player attacks, a probability of kill is calculated based on the relative strength of the attacker and defender, as well as a modifier based on each ship's type. The probability distribution is sampled, and the defending ship is deleted if destroyed. 

Detection range for two ships is calculated as the product of each's visibility coefficient and scouting coefficient. High visibility coefficients mean the ship is larger and will be more easily spotted by the enemy, while a higher scouting coefficient increases the detection range 

## Economy

Each turn, every port a player owns generates 25 raw materials. These raw materials are transformed into cash if the materials are transported to the home port by transport ships (capacity 100 raw materials per ship). A player is free to spend as much of their cash as desired per turn, or save for the next turn. Cash generated by a transport ship landing at the home port is available to spend on the current turn. 

Enemy ports are captured by positioning a LANDING ship adjacent to the port and issuing a `CapturePortAction`. New ports are built by positioning a BUILDER ship adjacent to an empty land tile and issuing a `BuildPortAction` targeting that tile; the builder is consumed and the tile becomes a new port owned by the buyer.

## Map

All distances are measured as manhattan distance. A simple yaml config is used to define top level map params, then a map is randomly created and stored in a json 

## Ship Types

- **Carrier, Battleship, Cruiser, Destroyer, Submarine** — combat ships.
- **Merchant** — transport. Carries up to 100 raw materials at a time; delivers cash when landing at the home port.
- **Landing** — captures enemy ports. While on a water tile adjacent to an enemy port, can issue a `CapturePortAction` targeting that port to flip its ownership.
- **Builder** — single-use. Move it to a water tile adjacent to empty (un-ported) land, then issue a `BuildPortAction` targeting that land tile. The builder is consumed and the tile becomes a new port owned by the buyer.

## Conventions

- **All distances are Manhattan distance** — movement, attack range, detection range, and build adjacency. "Adjacent" means Manhattan distance 1 (the four cardinal neighbors).
- **Ships exist only on water.** No ship ever occupies a land or port tile. All interactions with land (building a port, capturing a port, loading/unloading materials at a port) are performed via actions issued from an adjacent water tile. Ship type determines which of these actions are legal: LANDING can capture, BUILDER can build, MERCHANT can transfer materials.

## Building Ships

A player issues a `BuildShipAction` specifying:
- the friendly port to build at,
- the spawn tile (must be adjacent to that port, in bounds, water, and unoccupied),
- the ship type to build.

The action succeeds if the player owns the port, the spawn tile is legal, and the player can afford the ship's cost. Cost is deducted and the new ship appears at the spawn tile.

## Game Startup

At the start of every match:

1. **Map** — generated from `mapConfig.yaml` into `map.json` (random ports + land per player area). Each player additionally receives a fixed home port:
   - Player 0 home: `(0, 0)`
   - Player 1 home: `(width - 1, height - 1)`
2. **Starting cash** — both players begin with 250 cash.
3. **Starting ships** — none. Players must purchase their initial fleet from their starting cash on turn one.
4. **Turn order** — Player 0 acts first.

## Turn Mechanics

- Players take sequential turns. A turn ends when the current player issues an `EndTurnAction`.
- Movement is **per-tile**: each `MoveAction` advances one ship by one tile (4-directional). A ship can be moved up to its `speed` value worth of tiles per turn, across any number of `MoveAction`s. The agent receives a fresh observation after each tile, so it can react to newly-spotted enemies mid-traverse.
- A ship's `tiles_moved_this_turn` counter resets at the end of *its owner's* turn (not the opponent's).
- Ships can move and attack in either order within a turn.
